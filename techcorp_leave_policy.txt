1. Introduction

1.1 Purpose
This policy outlines the leave entitlements, procedures, and guidelines for all employees of TechCorp Industries. The company recognizes the importance of work-life balance and provides various types of leave to support employee wellbeing.

1.2 Scope
This policy applies to all full-time permanent employees of TechCorp Industries. Contractual and temporary employees should refer to their specific employment agreements.

1.3 Policy Owner
The Human Resources Department is responsible for administering and updating this policy.

2. Types of Leave

2.1 Casual Leave
Purpose: Short-term planned absences for personal matters, appointments, or urgent family needs.

Entitlement:
- All employees are entitled to 12 days of casual leave per calendar year
- Leave is credited at the beginning of the year (January 1st)
- Pro-rated for employees joining mid-year

Key Features:
- Can be taken in half-day increments (minimum 0.5 days)
- Maximum consecutive days: 3 days at a time
- Advance notice required: Minimum 1 working day (except emergencies)
- Cannot be carried forward to the next year
- Unused casual leave lapses on December 31st
- Can be combined with other leave types for extended absence

Application Process:
- Apply through the HR portal or inform your immediate supervisor
- Provide brief reason for leave
- Obtain approval before proceeding on leave

Approval Authority:
- Immediate supervisor/reporting manager
- HR approval required for applications exceeding 2 consecutive days

2.2 Sick Leave
Purpose: Medical illness, injury, or health-related appointments requiring absence from work.

Entitlement:
- All employees are entitled to 10 days of sick leave per calendar year
- Leave is credited at the beginning of the year
- Pro-rated for employees joining mid-year

Key Features:
- Can be taken without advance notice in case of sudden illness
- Maximum consecutive days: 7 days (beyond which medical certificate is mandatory)
- Medical certificate required: For absences of 3 or more consecutive days
- Cannot be carried forward to the next year
- Unused sick leave lapses on December 31st
- Emergency sick leave can be applied retroactively within 24 hours

Documentation Requirements:
- Medical certificate from a registered medical practitioner for absences exceeding 3 days
- Certificate must include: diagnosis, treatment dates, and fitness to resume work
- Submit medical documents within 48 hours of returning to work

Application Process:
- Inform supervisor as soon as possible (phone call/message acceptable for emergencies)
- Formal application through HR portal within 24 hours
- Submit medical certificate if applicable

Approval Authority:
- Immediate supervisor for applications up to 2 days
- HR and medical clearance required for extended sick leave

2.3 Earned Leave (Annual Leave/Privilege Leave)
Purpose: Extended breaks for rest, recreation, vacation, or personal matters requiring longer absence.

Entitlement:
Employees are entitled to earned leave based on tenure:
- 0-2 years of service: 18 days per year
- 2-5 years of service: 20 days per year
- 5+ years of service: 22 days per year
- Leave accrues monthly (1.5 to 1.83 days per month depending on tenure)

Key Features:
- Can be taken in half-day increments
- Maximum consecutive days: 15 days (subject to business requirements)
- Advance notice required: Minimum 7 working days
- Can be carried forward to the next year
- Maximum carry forward limit: 6 days (additional days lapse)
- Encashment: Up to 50% of unused earned leave can be encashed at year-end (subject to policy)

Planning and Scheduling:
- Employees should plan earned leave in advance
- Coordinate with team members to ensure business continuity
- Black-out periods may apply during critical business periods (communicated in advance)
- Popular vacation periods (holidays, year-end) require earlier application

Application Process:
- Apply through HR portal at least 7 days in advance
- Provide detailed dates and handover notes
- Obtain necessary approvals before booking travel

Approval Authority:
- Immediate supervisor and department head for leaves up to 7 days
- Senior management approval required for leaves exceeding 10 consecutive days

3. Leave Application Procedures

3.1 General Guidelines
Application Timeline:
- Plan and apply for leave well in advance
- Follow minimum notice periods as specified for each leave type
- Emergency situations will be considered on a case-by-case basis

Submission Process:
1. Log into the HR Employee Portal
2. Navigate to "Leave Management" section
3. Select leave type and enter dates
4. Provide reason and any supporting documents
5. Submit for approval
6. Wait for confirmation before proceeding on leave

Approval Workflow:
- Application routes to immediate supervisor
- Automatic escalation to HR if required
- Email notification sent upon approval/rejection
- Employees must receive written approval before proceeding on leave

3.2 What to Include in Leave Application
- Employee ID and name
- Leave type requested
- Start date and end date
- Total number of days
- Reason for leave (brief description)
- Contact information during leave (if applicable)
- Work handover plan (for extended leaves)

3.3 Emergency Leave Applications
In case of genuine emergencies where advance application is not possible:
- Inform supervisor immediately via phone/message
- Submit formal application within 24 hours
- Provide supporting documents within 48 hours of return
- Emergency leave is subject to retrospective approval

4. Leave Balance and Tracking

4.1 Checking Leave Balance
Employees can check their current leave balance through:
- HR Employee Portal (real-time updates)
- Monthly payslips
- Direct inquiry to HR Department

4.2 Leave Credit and Debit
- Credit: Leave is credited on January 1st each year (or pro-rated on joining date)
- Debit: Leave is deducted on the day(s) taken
- Real-time updates: Portal reflects current balance after each transaction

4.3 Negative Leave Balance
- Casual and sick leave cannot go into negative balance
- Applications will be rejected if sufficient balance is unavailable
- In exceptional cases, negative earned leave may be permitted (requires approval from HR Head)
- Negative balance must be cleared before year-end or upon separation

5. Leave Cancellation and Modification

5.1 Employee-Initiated Cancellation
- Employees can cancel approved leave through the HR portal
- Cancellation must be done at least 2 working days before the leave start date
- Leave balance will be restored immediately upon cancellation
- Last-minute cancellations (within 48 hours) require supervisor approval

5.2 Company-Initiated Cancellation
- In exceptional business circumstances, the company may request leave cancellation
- Reasonable advance notice will be provided
- Employees will be compensated appropriately
- Alternative leave dates will be offered

5.3 Modification of Dates
- Leave dates can be modified if requested before the start date
- Modifications require fresh approval
- Original application will be cancelled and new application created

6. Special Circumstances

6.1 Leave During Notice Period
- Employees serving notice period must obtain special approval for leave
- Earned leave can be availed subject to knowledge transfer completion
- Unused leave may be encashed as per separation policy

6.2 Leave During Probation
- Probationary employees are entitled to leave on a pro-rated basis
- Casual and sick leave available from joining date
- Earned leave accrues but usage requires manager approval

6.3 Public Holidays Falling During Leave
- Public holidays occurring during approved leave will not be counted as leave days
- Leave balance will be adjusted accordingly

6.4 Weekends During Leave
- Weekends falling within leave period are counted in total leave days
- Example: Friday + Weekend + Monday = 4 days leave (if Saturday-Sunday are offs)

7. Compliance and Violations

7.1 Unauthorized Absence
- Taking leave without approval is considered unauthorized absence
- May result in loss of pay and disciplinary action
- Repeated violations can lead to termination of employment

7.2 False Information
- Providing false information or fraudulent medical certificates is a serious offense
- Subject to immediate disciplinary action including termination

7.3 Leave Abuse
- Frequent casual leave applications immediately before/after weekends
- Pattern of sick leave on Mondays/Fridays without valid reason
- HR reserves the right to investigate suspicious leave patterns

8. Additional Leave Types (Beyond Scope of This System)
For completeness, employees should be aware of other leave types available (not covered in this system):
- Maternity Leave: 26 weeks (as per law)
- Paternity Leave: 15 days
- Bereavement Leave: Up to 5 days
- Compensatory Off: For working on scheduled offs
- Sabbatical Leave: For employees with 5+ years of service

Please refer to separate policy documents or contact HR for details on these leave types.
